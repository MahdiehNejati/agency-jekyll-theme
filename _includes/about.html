Resume

Places my robotics life has taken me so far:

  • 2015

    Argallab

    Professor Argall and her lab are changing how we think about assistive machines. The research here combines artificial intelligence, rehabilitation robotics and machine learning to find the perfect balance of shared control between the robot and the human. I get to work on extending the current capabilities of the smart powered wheelchair!

  • Summer 2015

    Applied Dexterity

    Applied Dexterity is an awesome startup, delivering surgical robotics platforms. I was their first official intern and I had the awesome opportunity to work on different aspects of the RAVEN-II surgical robot; from the mechanical design, electric circuit debugging, and software development. I also had the amazing opportunity to attended the Hamlyn Medical Robotics Symposium in London and see first-hand the latest is medical robotics research!

  • 2014-2015

    Northwestern University

    I joined the 2014 Master of Science in Robotics cohort. This program has given me the opportunity to expand my technical knowledge and skills in numerous branches of robotics engineering. I got to volunteer at IROS and ROSCON 2015. I was an honorary reportor for RoboticsTomorrow at the Automate Tradeshow 2015. Our cohort had the opportunity to attend the National Resturant Tradeshow and talk about how robotics can change the future of the food industry. We also got to exhibit our projects at the Museum of Science and Industry!

  • 2013

    StretchSense

    I got to make cool stuff with stretchy sensors.

  • 2010-2012

    The Biomimetics Lab

    I started as an intern in the lab after recieving a summer research scholarship. This was when I found out how work can be super fun when you are surounded by amazing people doing amazing work. I learned so much and had a ton of fun working on my pneumatically actuated robotic fin project.

  • 2008-2012

    The University of Auckland

    I gradued with a degree in Mechatronics Engineering with first class honors! I owe a lot to my undergraduate experience and all the people I met at UoA.
